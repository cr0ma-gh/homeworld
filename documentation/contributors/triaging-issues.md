# Triaging Issues

In a volunteer-run project such as this one, we need to have rules to process incoming contributions, and specifically those that come in the form of [Issues] or [Merge Requests] (MR).

It is not an easy task, and this section vastly remains TODO.
Here's why: You need to balance a few things.

- Users want their issues fixed **but** you don't want to [burn out your contributors][contributor care].
- New contributors want their fix/feature merged **but** you want to make sure it does not introduce more bugs and maintenance.
- Fixing issues and reviewing MRs takes time, **but** leaving too many of them open undermines contributor morale, and repels users and new contributors (they think they won't be listened to).

There is generally no rule of thumb, and addressing issues and MRs is much more of a people skill than a coding skill.
You need to understand the needs and constraints of both sides to help things go forward.

## Project Direction

Having a clear vision of where we want things to go helps a lot in taking decisions.
Having a single person in charge of that vision, a "Benevolant Dictator for Life" (BDfL) is a very simple solution to that problem, but one that is fundamentally incompatible with a community-run project.

We want the Gardens of Kadesh project to be owned by the people who make it.
As such, the rules presented here MUST be discussed when contributors come and go (and from time to time), and MUST evolve to reflect the general direction that we collectively want to take.

1. Retain the look & feel of the original game from 1999
1. Have as little bugs or crashes as possible
1. Support as many platforms as possible (within the limits of what contributors are willing to test)
1. Add quality of life improvements
1. Make it as easy to mod as possible
