## Tracking Issues

For the purposes of this documentation, an "issue" is any item that at least one member of the community (including contributors themselves) would like addressed by the contributors.
This includes bugs, features, but also questions, or missing pieces of documentation.

It is important to
1. Give channels to the community to raise issues (like Github, Gitlab, [Discord] or [matrix])
2. Track those issues such that they are not forgotten by contributors or simply buried under a lot of text (as can too easily happen in instant-communication channels like Discord)
3. Gather those issues in a central place to help triage them

Currently, that central place is [the Gitlab issues] because it is right next to the main repo, and has decent tools to tag and sort issues.

### Opening an Issue

You have found an issue with the game, the build system, the documentation, or any other aspect of the project?
Or maybe you have a specific question you'd like to ask the contributors?

**The best way to let us know is to [open an issue on our Gitlab][the gitlab issues].**
(You will need an account on either Gitlab.com, GitHub.com, Bitbucket.org, Salesforce.com, or Google.com)

If that does not suit you, for whatever reason, you may also tell us about your issue on [Discord], [[matrix]], or via [e-mail].
We will create the issue on your behalf on Gitlab (anonymizing it if you wish).
