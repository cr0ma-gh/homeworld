# Contributing to GoK

There are many ways to help the project. Here is a non-exhaustive list, in no particular order:

- Talk about it around you! Especially about our [open letter to Gearbox][open letter]!
- [Improve the documentation](/documentation/README.md#helping-with-documentation)
- [Report bugs and crashes][open an issue]
- [Suggest ideas for improvements][open an issue]
- [Moderate & triage issues/ideas](/documentation/contributors/triaging-issues.md)
- [Fix bugs and crashes](/documentation/contributors/contributing-code.md)
- [Suggest new things to add to this list!][open an issue]

We do not accept monetary donations because it could be seen as an attempt to make profit.
([Why is that a problem?](/documentation/contributors/license-agreement.md))

You may donate your time by helping with the items listed above, or hardware to test the game on or host code and binaries on.

[open letter]: https://gardens-of-kadesh.gitlab.io/#request_for_an_unambiguous_license
[open an issue]: /documentation/community/README.md#opening-an-issue

## Governance

There are currently (2025-09-16) no rules set in stone.
There are only 3 active maintainers (give or take 2), so we just apply care and common sense to try to keep things rolling.

If you are interested in joining us, there is room for everyone, regardless of age, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, disability, economic status **or proficiency with English, software programming, or computers in general**.

The only people we will actively reject are racists, bigots, and other people intolerant of the inclusivity stated above.
(We've never had any incident so far.)

Chances are, if you contribute a few things, hang around, and generally show involvement, (and ask nicely) we will have no issue giving you admin access to the repository, Discord, or other hosted tool we use.
(In other words: The basic human social interaction of mutual trust.)

## Contributor Care

**Contributor time is volunteer time, and it is finite.**

The time we put into this project is our free time.
We are not paid for it, and it is taken from time we could have spent doing other things (like gaming, reading, taking a walk, hanging out with friends, etc.).

Contributing should be done happily, because you enjoy the time you spend doing it.
The health of the contributor goes above all else.
Please care for yourself and for others.

Protect yourself and other contributors from burn-out.
You can (gently) remind players and other members of the community of that fact when you spot a situation that could harm yourself or an other contributor.

(Like someone being too insistant because they don't get an answer fast enough, or complaining that the bug they found has not been fixed yet. It does not mean that person is a bad person, but it is called harassment, and it *does* harm.)
